Exotic dance moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate worl, I did study exotic tribal dances. To answer your question, my favorite dance are as follows, with definitions:

Polynesian chicken dance
A little know but very influential dance dating as far as 300BS, a whole village would dance around in a circle like chickens, to encourage their livestock or be "fruitful".
Icelandic Brownian shuffle
Before the Icelanders developed fire as a means fo getting warm, they used to practice this dance, which involves hudding close together in a circle on the floor, and shuffing their bodies around in imperceptable tiny, very rapid movements. One of my fellows students used to say that he thought this dance inspires modern styles such as Twerking.
Artic robot dance
An interesting example of historic misinformation, English explorers in the 1960s believe to have discovered a new dance style characterised by "robotic", stilled movements, being practices by inhabitants of Northern Alaska and Canada. Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this because they were really cold.
